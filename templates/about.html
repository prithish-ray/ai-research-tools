Background

Investment Research Foundation

My background is in fundamental equity research: company analysis, financial statement analysis, valuation, competitive positioning, and investment thesis construction. This is not a background in general machine learning or software engineering — it is a background in the specific analytical problems that AI tools need to solve to be genuinely useful in an investment context.

That distinction matters. Most AI tools applied to investing are built by engineers who do not understand the research workflow. The result is tools that are technically functional but intellectually shallow — they produce fluent text without addressing the real bottlenecks: structured judgement, assumption tracking, source discipline, and decision auditability.

I build tools that address those bottlenecks because I have experienced them as an analyst.

Work

What I Build

AI-assisted research systems: tools that embed structured investment frameworks into LLM workflows, enforce source discipline, surface limitations, and produce outputs that analysts can actually use and audit — rather than outputs they must simply trust.

Every tool I build is deployed, version-controlled, and documented. I ship to Render from GitHub. I use Python, Flask, FastAPI, Groq, and Tavily. I write structured prompt chains, not single-shot LLM queries. I build for repeatability, not demos.

Positioning

How I Think About AI in Investment Research

AI does not solve the hard problem in equity research. The hard problem is structured judgement under uncertainty, with accountability for the decision. AI solves a different problem: it reduces the cost of information retrieval, pattern recognition, and first-draft synthesis.

The risk is not that AI makes analysts lazy. The risk is that AI makes plausible-sounding outputs so cheap to produce that analysts stop interrogating them. The tools I build are designed to resist that failure mode: they surface sources, flag uncertainty, separate retrieval from reasoning, and always label outputs as requiring analyst review.

I am not positioning these tools as investment advice engines. I am building research workflow infrastructure — the equivalent of a well-designed checklist or research template, but automated and auditable.
